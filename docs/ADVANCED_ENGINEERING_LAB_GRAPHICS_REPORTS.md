# Workbench v0.7.3: Advanced Engineering, Lab Science, Physical Systems, and Report Exports

This release expands the Workbench into a broader serious tool stack while preserving strict educational and professional-review boundaries.

## Added calculator areas

- Nuclear Physics Calculator: decay, decay constants, binding energy, dose proxy, mean lifetime.
- Particle Physics Calculator: relativistic energy/momentum, event-rate estimates, detector-resolution proxy, lifetime-width relation.
- Neurophysics Calculator: membrane RC, Nernst potential, integrate-and-fire proxy, conduction delay.
- Rocket Science and Orbital Mechanics Calculator: delta-v, thrust-to-weight, orbital velocity, required mass ratio.
- Electronics Engineering Calculator: Ohm/power, RC filters, RLC resonance, op-amp gain, ADC resolution.
- RF and Antenna Calculator: wavelength, free-space path loss, link budget, gain conversion.
- Full-Stack Engineering Tool: FMEA, factor of safety, reliability curves, engineering maturity.
- Lab Science Calculator: serial dilution, CFU counts, qPCR efficiency, dose-response research curves, replicate QA/QC.
- Clinical Research Calculator: diagnostic metrics, NNT, odds ratio, effect size.

## Graphics and report outputs

The frontend now adds export controls to calculated results:

- Download graph as SVG.
- Download graph as high-resolution PNG via browser canvas conversion.
- Download result JSON.
- Generate a PDF-ready report view using browser print/save-as-PDF.

SVG remains the primary graph format because it scales cleanly in WordPress, PDF, and publication contexts.

## Boundaries

The Workbench does not provide weapons design, hazardous-material procedures, medical diagnosis, treatment instructions, launch/flight certification, RF law bypassing, or safety-critical professional certification. Outputs are educational and analytical support only.
