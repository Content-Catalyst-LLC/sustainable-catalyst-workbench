# Workbench v0.7.0: Global Impact, Environmental Monitoring, Law, Health, Earth/Ocean/Climate, and Metaphysics

This release adds a serious public-systems cluster to Sustainable Catalyst Workbench. The intent is to avoid lightweight calculators for high-stakes topics and instead provide transparent analytical scaffolds, assumptions, warnings, and visual outputs.

## New tool cluster

- Environmental Monitoring QA/QC Tool
- Global Impact Assessment Matrix
- Climate Change Scenario Tool
- Earth Science Hazard Analyzer
- Marine Biology and Ocean Health Tool
- Astronomy Calculator
- Materials Science Calculator
- Health and Medical/Public Health Analytics Tool
- International Law Issue Mapper
- Legal Traditions Comparator
- Metaphysics Framework Tool

## Serious-tool principles

1. Every high-stakes tool includes visible disclaimers.
2. Environmental monitoring tools include data-quality and threshold logic, not just a single score.
3. Global impact tools combine environmental, health, legal, rights, governance, equity, reversibility, and uncertainty dimensions.
4. Health tools are public-health analytics only and do not provide diagnosis, treatment, or clinical triage.
5. Legal tools are issue-spotting and comparative learning tools, not legal advice.
6. Metaphysics tools structure inquiry and interpretation rather than pretending to calculate truth.

## Backend orientation

Python powers the current deterministic analytics and SVG graphs. R, Julia, Haskell, C++, GIS, climate, and domain-specific engines are represented as extension pathways for future production-grade specialist modules.
